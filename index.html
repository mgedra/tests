Learning methods

Teaching methods are the broader techniques used to help students achieve learning outcomes, while activities are the different ways of implementing these methods. Teaching methods help students: master the content of the course. learn how to apply the content in particular contexts.

The SQ3R Method Retrieval Practice Spaced Practice
The SQ3R method is a reading comprehension technique that helps students identify important facts and retain information within their textbook. SQ3R (or SQRRR) is an acronym that stands for the five steps of the reading comprehension process. Retrieval practice is based on the concept of remembering at a later time. Recalling an answer to a question improves learning more than looking for the answer in your textbook. And, remembering and writing down the answer to a flashcard is a lot more effective than thinking you know the answer and flipping the card over early. Spaced practice encourages students to study over a longer period of time instead of cramming the night before. When our brains almost forget something, they work harder to recall that information. Spacing out your studying allows your mind to make connections between ideas and build upon the knowledge that can be easily recalled later.

How to tell what your learning style is?

Knowing what your learning style is can help you succeed at school and in training situations. By being aware of what your style is, you can seek out methods of learning and studying that suit your style. Follow these steps to determine your learning type:

  1. Consider your interests and preferences.
  2. Think about how you study.
  3. Reflect on your best learning experiences.
    • Seek your teachers.
    • Think about what kind of learning has helped you the best.
    • Focus on your strenghts and weeknesses.
  4. Determine which learning style you identify with the most.
